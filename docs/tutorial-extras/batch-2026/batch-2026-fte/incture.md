## Round 1: System Design Test (Written Round ‚Äì 45 Minutes)  

### Details about the Round  
Candidates were given **two system design questions** and had to choose **one** to answer in detail.  

### Questions Asked  

1. **Design a High-Level Design (HLD) for Paytm**  
2. **Design an Application for Housing Society / Residential Complex Management**  

#### Problem Statement (Brief)  
In residential societies, residents face issues managing daily utilities and services such as garbage collection, water supply, and grocery needs. There is no unified platform for raising complaints, requesting services, or tracking updates, causing communication gaps and inefficiencies.  

The goal was to design a **mobile app/website** that allows residents to:  
- Raise service requests and complaints  
- Track their status  
- Communicate with management  
- Access essential utilities  

---

### My Answer  

I wrote the answers **clearly and comprehensively**, covering:  
- **Chosen tech stack and reasoning**  
- **Alternative technologies**  
- **Architecture and scalability** considerations  

Because of the clarity and depth in my explanation, the interviewers **did not ask any follow-up questions** from my design sheet.  

#### Example (My Approach)  
- **Frontend:** Flutter ‚Äì chosen for its **cross-platform capability**, reducing both cost and time (works for Android, iOS, and Web).  
- **ML Model:** Used **CNN** for recommendations (automated pattern recognition).  
- **Backend:** **FastAPI** for its **auto-documentation** and Python compatibility (easy synchronization with ML models).  
- **Authentication:** Included login/signup with user roles.  
- **Chatting Feature:** Added for customer service support.  
- **Admin Panel:** Displays user logs and status updates.  
- **Containerization:** Used **Docker** to make the app scalable.  

Also added features like **rating system**, **feedback module**, and **flowchart diagrams** to explain user‚Äìsystem interaction.  

> üí° **Tip for Incture System Design Round:**  
> Always include multiple features, ML components, and a rating system. Mention tech stack reasoning and alternatives.
