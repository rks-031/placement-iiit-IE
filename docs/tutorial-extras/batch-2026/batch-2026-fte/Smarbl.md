# Smarbl Interview Experience  
**Article by**: Saswat 
**Role**: Intern + SDE  
**Company**: Smarbl   
**Mode**: Virtual (Online)  

---

## Round 1: Phone Screening Round (30 minutes)

**Format**:  
- Conducted by one of the co-founders.  
- Focused on resume-based discussion and technical fundamentals.  

**Content Covered**:  
- **Self Introduction (2 minutes)**: Internship experience, technical interests, projects, and technologies used.  
- **Project Discussion**:  
  - Detailed questions about a **low-level implementation of a trading engine in C++**.  
  - Focus on trade order handling, acceptance, and processing.  
- **C++ Concepts**:  
  - Memory management, padding, offset, smart pointers.  
  - Questions tested understanding of memory layout and efficiency.  
- **Linux Fundamentals**:  
  - Basic commands and concepts.  
  - Comparison question: **Why ‘mv’ is faster than ‘cp’** (testing file system understanding).  

**Feedback**:  
- The interviewer appreciated the clarity of explanations and technical reasoning.  
- Positive remarks at the end of the round.  

---

## Round 2: Technical Interview (1 hour)

**Panel**: Conducted by two other co-founders.  
**Mode**: Screen-sharing and live discussion.  

---

### **Part 1: Project Deep Dive**

- Candidate was asked to present the **trading engine project** in detail.  
- Discussion included:  
  - Design structure and motivation.  
  - Implementation details and logic behind architectural choices.  
  - Real-time walkthrough of codebase.  
- **Cross-Questions**:  
  - Why specific design choices were made.  
  - Alternative approaches and trade-offs.  
- **Demonstration**:  
  - Candidate successfully ran the trading engine live, showing a completed trade execution.  

---

### **Part 2: Core Technical Questions**

**Topics Covered**:  
- **Operating System & Linux**:  
  - Zombie and orphan processes.  
  - Forking and killing processes.  
  - Range of PIDs.  
  - Filesystem types.  
  - Comparison between Windows and Linux internals.  

- **Internship Discussion**:  
  - Candidate was asked to describe contributions in previous internship projects.  
  - Focus on problem-solving approach and teamwork.  

---

### **Part 3: Behavioural Questions**

**Examples Asked**:  
- How do you **prioritize work** under tight deadlines?  
- What are your **views on vibe coding** and team collaboration?  
- How would you **handle project failures** close to delivery?  

**Candidate’s Queries**:  
- Asked about company’s **tech stack**, development process, and domain specialization.  

**Feedback**:  
- The interviewers were friendly, professional, and encouraging.  
- Feedback was largely positive.  

---

## Conclusion

Smarbl’s interview process focused on:  
- **Project depth and real implementation understanding**.  
- **Strong command over C++ and memory management**.  
- **Solid Linux fundamentals and OS knowledge**.  
- **Clear communication and reasoning under pressure**.  

After a few working days, the candidate **received an offer** from Smarbl.  

---

**Key Takeaways**:  
- Be confident about your **projects and their internals**.  
- Revise **C++ memory concepts** and **OS basics**.  
- Prepare to **explain and defend your design choices** logically.  
- Maintain **clarity and calmness** during technical discussions.
