**Job Description**: Trainee Ordinate  
**Mode**: Online  

---

## Resume Screening

- There was **no Online Assessment Round**.  
- Shortlisting was done **purely based on resume screening**.  
- I applied for the **ML Engineer role**.  
- There were **three different roles**, involving a mix of Development and Machine Learning.  
- Resumes were evaluated based on **relevance to the respective roles**.

---

## Technical Round 1 (L1)

**Duration:** 30 minutes  

### DSA
- Sort all the zeroes to the end of an array **without changing the original order** of other elements.

### CS Fundamentals

**OOPS:**
- What is polymorphism?  
- Types of polymorphism  
- Deep copy vs shallow copy  
- Garbage management in Python  

### Projects
- My projects were mainly based on **Machine Learning and Deep Learning**.  
- Asked to explain different **CNN architectures**.  
- Detailed explanation of my ML/DL projects.  
- Majority of the questions were **resume-based**.

## Technical Round 3 (Final Round)

**Duration:** 30 minutes  

- **No DSA questions** were asked.  
- This round was heavily focused on **real-life, case-based problem solving**.  
- The interviewer selected one of my **time-series forecasting projects** and framed multiple scenario-based questions aligned with **Big Billion Days** use cases.

### Key Case-Based Questions
1. How do external factors such as **price changes, promotional events, and holidays** impact multivariate forecasting models?  
2. Apart from sales, what other parameters determine the **success of a marketing campaign**?  
3. How do you handle **Twitter data** for analysis?  
   - Follow-up: Explain **sentiment analysis**.  
4. How would you identify the **top reviews** using a recommender system based on **cosine similarity** stored in a database?  
5. Why canâ€™t cosine similarity be applied directly to **raw text**, and how do you **vectorize text** for similarity computation?

At the end of the interview, I was asked about my **aspirations and long-term goals** in this domain.

---

## Key Takeaways

- A **strong and relevant resume** is critical, as shortlisting was entirely resume-based.  
- **Core fundamentals** (OOPS, DBMS, Python) and **project depth** matter a lot.  
- **Scenario-based ML problem-solving** is heavily tested, especially applying models to real business use cases.  
- Speak with **confidence** and keep the interviewer actively engaged.
