# Medibuddy Interview Experience (SDE Internship | On-Campus)  
**Article by**: Aman Jaiswal  

---

## Eligibility  
Students with **CGPA 8.5+** were shortlisted for the first round.  
Around **30 students** qualified for the Online Assessment.

---

## Round 1: Online Assessment (HackerEarth)

The OA consisted of **3 DSA questions (Mediumâ€“Hard level)** hosted on HackerEarth.  
The problems focused on:

- Arrays  
- Graphs  
- Stack-based logic  

After evaluation, **only 8 students** were shortlisted for the next round.

---

## Round 2: Technical Interview (Virtual on Google Meet)

The round started with:

- A brief introduction by the interviewer about **Medibuddy and its products**  
- Followed by **my self-introduction**  

Then, I was given **2 medium-level DSA problems** to solve on a shared editor.

---

### **Problem 1: Maximum Width of Ramp**  
*(Stack-based problem | LeetCode)*  

- First explained the **brute-force approach**  
- Then optimized using a **stack-based approach**  
- Missed an edge case initially, interviewer guided me  
- Corrected the issue and successfully completed the solution  

---

### **Problem 2: Geekâ€™s Village and Wells**  
*(Graph + Multi-Source BFS | GFG)*  

- Immediately recognized it as a **multi-source BFS** problem  
- Explained the entire approach and dry-ran it  
- Started coding, but due to time constraints, interviewer asked for a summary of the remaining part  

Finally, I asked questions about **Clara**, an internal tool used at Medibuddy.  
This concluded the round.

---

## Round 3: Technical + Managerial Round

Only **5 out of 8** students were shortlisted for this round.  
It included **CS fundamentals + project discussion**.

---

### Topics Covered:

#### **Operating System**
- Multithreading vs Multitasking  
- Scheduling  
- Deadlocks  

#### **DBMS**
- Sharding vs Partitioning  
- Transactions  

#### **OOPs & Java**
- Garbage Collection  
- Inheritance  
- Polymorphism  

#### **System Design**
- Basic concepts  

---

### **Project Discussion**

The interviewer asked me to:

- Show code and file structure  
- Explain:  
  - Why specific packages & middlewares were used  
  - Purpose of hooks in the architecture  
  - End-to-end project flow  
  - How I would **debug an authentication error** in production  

After this thorough discussion, the interview ended.  
There was **no separate HR round**.

## Tip

Focus on **core DSA** topics like:

- Stack
